Eye Care Services

Comprehensive vision exams and so much more — from routine eye care to the management of complex eye conditions.

Service

Comprehensive Eye Exams

Complete eye exams are not just for updating your prescription. At Macy's Eye Care, our exams are designed to screen for diseases and identify any eye abnormalities that could affect your vision and overall health.

During your visit, our optometrists will check your visual acuity, eye coordination, color vision, peripheral vision, and eye pressure. We also use advanced diagnostic technology to examine the structures of your eye in detail.

What we screen for:

  • Glaucoma and elevated eye pressure
  • Cataracts and lens clouding
  • Macular degeneration
  • Diabetic retinopathy
  • Retinal conditions and detachment risk
  • Dry eye syndrome
  • Refractive errors (nearsightedness, farsightedness, astigmatism)

Advanced Diagnostic Technology

Optomap Retinal Imaging

Ultra-widefield retinal imaging for a comprehensive view of your retinal health without dilation in most cases.

Slit Lamp Examination

Detailed examination of your cornea, lens, iris, and other eye structures using specialized microscopy.

Autorefractor

Precise measurement of your refractive error to guide your prescription with accuracy.

Tonometry (Puffer Test)

Measurement of eye pressure to screen for glaucoma risk.

Pediatric & Infant Eye Exams

Specialized exams for children's visual development — early detection matters.

Conditions We Treat

Glaucoma

The "sneak thief of sight" — often symptomless until significant damage occurs. Regular screening is essential.

Dry Eye Syndrome

Treatment options including warm compress therapy, tea tree oil lid scrubs, and artificial tear management.

Cataracts

Monitoring and co-management of cataracts and cataract surgery referrals when appropriate.

Diabetic Retinopathy

Annual dilated eye exams are critical for diabetic patients to detect retinal changes early.

Macular Degeneration

Monitoring of AMD and counseling on nutrition and lifestyle factors that affect progression.

Eye Condition Treatment

Whether you have been diagnosed with a chronic eye condition or are experiencing new symptoms, our team provides expert care and ongoing management to protect your sight.

Common conditions we manage:

  • Glaucoma

  • Cataracts
  • Diabetic Eye Disease
  • Age-Related Macular Degeneration (AMD)
  • Pink Eye (Conjunctivitis)
  • Eye Allergies

Emergency Eye Care Services

We offer emergency services that require immediate and urgent eye care. If your eye is injured, don't try to judge the severity of it — seek professional care right away.

Call us immediately at 808-492-5066 if you experience any of the symptoms listed. Use your best judgment — if you feel your issue is truly life-threatening, visit your nearest emergency treatment center.

Call Now: 808-492-5066

Things NOT to do while waiting for care: Do not press on the injured eye, do not rub the eye(s), do not attempt to remove an embedded object, and do not use dry cotton or sharp instruments on the eye.

Emergency Symptoms

Seek care immediately for:

  • Sudden loss of vision
  • Sudden double vision
  • Sudden red or painful eye
  • Pink Eye
  • New onset flashes and/or floaters
  • Foreign body in the eye (especially metal or chemicals)
  • New or worsening swelling or pain after eye surgery
  • Eye trauma or injury

Children's Vision Milestones

Infants (Birth–1 year)

First eye exam recommended at 6 months to ensure eyes are tracking correctly and developing normally.

Preschool (Ages 2–5)

Vision screening to detect amblyopia (lazy eye), strabismus, and other early vision problems before school age.

School Age (Ages 6–18)

Annual exams recommended. Uncorrected vision problems can impact learning, reading, and school performance.

Myopia Management

Progressive myopia in children is increasingly common. Early intervention can help slow progression.

Children's Eye Care

Early detection of vision problems is critical for children's development. Many learning difficulties in school are actually undiagnosed vision problems — a child may not know what "normal" vision looks like.

Our team provides comprehensive pediatric and infant eye care exams in a welcoming environment. We make kids feel comfortable and help parents understand what to look for between visits.

Signs your child may have a vision problem:

  • Squinting or closing one eye to see
  • Sitting too close to the TV or screen
  • Frequent headaches after reading
  • Avoiding reading or near-work activities
  • Short attention span for visual tasks
  • Eyes that turn in, out, up, or down
  • Tilting head or covering one eye
